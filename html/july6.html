Is there anything worse in the world than going back into work after a long holdiay weekend? I mean, yes I'm sure there are, but first world problems people!

Katie and I did not ride into work together like we usually do. She doesn't work the same place as me, but I drive by her job on the way to mine so I usually drop her off. Then I pick her up when I get off. But today she went in a little later and will be getting off a little bit later.

Seth, our son, has a 2009 Dodge Avenger with over 160k miles on it. Despite it's age and general look it has been running pretty well. Last year he had an issue with it overheating after he hit a deer. We replaced the radiator and piping but it was still over heating so he took it to a mechanic.

He said he was struggling to make it even short trips of less than 10 miles because it kept overheating on him. He also had to run it with the heater on full blast to keep it from overheating. Of course he didn't tell me that he has been having issues until his mother tells me he needs to take it into a shop.

Normally I would suggest that we look at it and possibly see about fixing it ourselves, but with the heat I dunno, it's probably best if someone else takes a quick look. Plus it's important for him to learn what the upkeep on a car is like. Also, when it's his only vehicle we need to get it fixed because he isn't sure what the problem really is.

As a result Katie has been going into work a little later and staying a little later each day. This is so she can drop Seth off at work in the mornings and pick him up in the evenings. That means that each evening I will have about an extra 2 hours to myself.

Grid Cube

I recieved the completed files for GridCube on Friday afternoon. Unfortunately due to the power outage I wasn't able to do anything with them. I started on Saturday evening though when the power did come back on. I've completed printing one side of the cube, and it looks really good. I can't wait to complete it.

I assume that the designers of the printer are from some Metric centered country. The recommended screws for the model are M3X20mm screws. I checked at 3 different hardware stores, Lowes, Home Depot, and Do It Best, and none of them had metric screws. I could get metric bolts, and nuts, but not screws.

I did some googling and the closest imperial size is a #4 x 3/4" screw. I think. I ordered a 100 pack from Home Depot. The size on the print isn't specific and they are not threaded, so it should work, I just wish it was easier to find metric things in America.

I also need to find some 11" x 11" acrylic so I can make one side clear. I need to look at all the options available and come up with some additional layouts. I worry that the cubes, while awesome looking and strong, they take a lot of filament to print. I would assume each cube will take 1 - 2 full rolls of filament to make. If that is the case I may have to make them just solid colors like black.
