It's Hard Being a Parent

I'm sure there is an argument to be made that the things I'm about to discuss are self-imposed. Afterall I did raise the kid so any of the behaviors he may have are my own making. Some of that may be true, but anyone who is a parent knows that sometimes a decision is made out of convenience or laziness, not always because it's the right thing to do in the moment. Sometimes things just happen without us noticing and then precedent is set. And YES, if you are a parent it is like being a lawyer and precedent can be set!

My son graduated from high school about a month ago. We are very proud of him, but furhter than that he finished with certifications for collision repair and was able to join the workforce as skilled labor. He has a job in collision repair and seems to like it. He has a set schedule, full time, and the such. The downside is that he shot himself in the foot with pay. They asked him during the interview what he wanted and his response was "I dunno man, $13 an hour?". Which in his defense is more than he has ever made, but is very low for the work he is doing.

This brings us to a problem he is going through. Katie looked at his debit card habits and noticed that he was spending money at Sheetz almost every day. When asked he stated he was buying drinks and snacks every day. Additionally he is buying drinks and food at Dairy Queen, the closest restaurant to where he works, almost every day as well.

Katie would make him lunch everyday if I would allow her, but I had to draw the line. He's an adult working full time, he does no other chores around the house except his own laundry. He is not going to have his Mommy make him lunch too. Maybe I'm being too harsh, I dunno, but I feel like doing these kinds of things is how you coddle your child and they never mature into a man. Or they never become truly independent. I know he's only 18, but when I was his age I had my own apartment and never looked back.

In an effort to help him we decided to take him to Costco and Sams Club so he could buy a whole weeks, or more worth of food. This should prevent him from spending more money than he needs to and keeps him from buying junk he doesn't need. I explained to him, he is buying an energy drink from Sheetz daily for $3.50 - $4 a piece. A case of 24 energy drinks is $40 and should last him all month and literally saves him $40.

All that went relatively fine, the part I have a problem is when we left the house he was sitting in the back of the truck with headphones in. Not talking. When we got to the store he kept his headphones in. Not talking. We were shopping for food for him specifically and every time I asked him a question he had to take his headphones out. Like DUDE WTF. Take them out! Then he acted like he didn't want to be there and we were inconveniencing him. He's lucky Katie is as forgiving and loving as she is because I would have went home over the headphone thing alone.

The headphones is a generational issue for his generation. I'm sure we have all been in public and seen a kid with those things in, even when working a customer facing job acting like it's 100% normal to ignore half of what is going on. The other thing is, when he got a little older and wanted to listen to his music in headphones on road trips and things you think nothing of it. It's not a big deal and every one is a little happier that way. Unfortunately it sets the precedent though and here we are where he has the damned things in all the time and we let it slide because it all started on a road trip.

New Grill

The bright side to the trip, aside from saving my son lots of money even if he doesn't appreciate it, is that I got a new grill! The one I had I've had for a few years and I purchased it in part because it had a smoke box. A few weeks ago I spent 12 hours smoking a brisket and it was 12 hours of constant hands on monitoring, feeding, and playing to smoke it. And you know what? It was trash! I couldn't keep the temperature stable enough to have a long low smoke and it was over cooked and dry.

The one I had also lacked a thermometer so I had to buy this blue tooth one and it's frankly annoying. You would think that it would be convenient because it was blue tooth, but any time I went inside it disconnected, so was less useful than a standard thermometer. There were a lot of little things that added up to a less than good time when using the grill.

I've been eyeing this one at Sams for a while though and I'm glad I got it. The quality of this grill is way better than the one I had. I know you think that a generic brand grill wouldn't scream quality but it's nice. The steel is thick, the cast iron grates are thick, and it came with a cover.

I put it together outside and seasoned it, then grilled some potatoes, chicken legs, and steaks on it. They all turned out really well and were delicios!

Rough Idle Mower

When Katie and I bought our house we knew we would need a riding mower. We have 2/3 of an acre, and while we could push mow the whole thing, I don't want to! We purcahsed a used mower off this old couple on Facebook Market Place for $500 last year, and so far it has been running pretty well. It loses air in the back right tire, but as long as I keep it topped off it's fine.

Last week though I noticed that the mower seemed to be running rougher than usual. I thought it was because I let the grass get a little longer between mows. This was because of rain as much as laziness. So I finished and parked it. Yesterday though I had to mow again. This thing sounded rough! I checked the oil and it was low so I topped it off, and that seemed to help a little. I then mowed the whole yard, but toward the end that thing was popping and banging! I was worried I wouldn't be able to finish it.

A more rational level headed person may have not mowed at all, and did some more troubleshooting to figure out what the problem was. I wanted to get the yard done though. Additionally, I've been thinking of getting a new mower since the new year started. They cost about $2000 for a Craftsman or Murray. Katie has not been into this idea t all though so I haven't really pushed it.

A quick google search showed that if it's running rough it may be due to 3 reasons though.

  1. Dirty or old Fuel. - This seems unlikely as I just added gas from my 5 gallon gas tank prior to starting it up.
  2. Spark Plug - I've ordered a replacement online. I'm hoping it's this one.
  3. Carburetor - I'm hoping its not this. Mostly because I've never done anything like this before.

I'll have to keep you informed.
